Introduction

This GeoExplorer example lets you explore the official cartographic information of Castellbisbal. Take a look at the associated documentation in the left tree, or load one of the predefined map views from the central list. The map viewer will let you play with the cartograply interactively, download or print views, locate and get extra information from elements, and manipulate the layer tree to adjust the map composition to your needs.

How to explore the maps

Toolbar

Choose the action you want to perform over the map:

Previous view:
Undo map view changes.
Vista siguiente:
Recover map view changes.
Pan map:
Click & drag to recenter map view. Use mouse wheel to change zoom.
Feature Info:
Click over an element to get its properties.
Measure:
Draw a line or a polygon on the map and measure its length or area.
Print map:
Download a capture of actual map in a printable or image format. Choose between layouts, formats and resolutions.

Search tabs

Localize elements and show them on the map. There are many search widgets:

Street Directory:
Choose a streetname and a portal from the municipal street database.
Placenames:
Search across the official place names. Type some text to filter placenames starting with it, or open drop down to show the full list.
Equipment:
Search for a municipal facility. Choose a category first, then select the facility in the second drop-down.
UTM:
Center map view to a precise location typing a coordinate pair in UTM projection (fuse 31, North hemisphere, European Datum 1950).
Cadastre:
Locate a cadastral parcel from the Catastro Virtual Office through its official cadastral reference code.

Layer tree

Manage base layers and overlays. Add or remove layers from view, either from own map services or third party services, activate or deactivate layer view, drawing order, and other layer properties.

Legend panel

Shows a schematic description of active layers simbology.

Main map

Shows the actual map data from the collection of active layers.

Reference map

Place the main map visible area into a broader geographical context.
